Ratangad Fort

Ratangad Fort, located in the Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra, is a historic fort known for its natural beauty and adventurous trekking routes. It is perched atop the Sahyadri mountain range and offers breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes.

The fort is believed to be over 2000 years old and is closely associated with the Maratha Empire. It features ancient caves, water cisterns, and the iconic "Nedhe" or eye of the needle, a natural rock formation.

Ratangad Fort is surrounded by lush greenery and is a popular trekking destination. The fort also offers panoramic views of the Bhandardara backwaters and nearby peaks like Kalsubai.

Today, Ratangad Fort is a favorite among trekkers and history enthusiasts. Its historical significance and scenic beauty make it a must-visit site in Maharashtra.
