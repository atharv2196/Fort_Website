Rajgad Fort

Rajgad Fort, located near Pune in Maharashtra, is one of the most significant forts in Maratha history. It served as the capital of the Maratha Empire under Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj for over 25 years.

The fort is perched at an altitude of 4,514 feet and is known for its strategic location and architectural brilliance. It is divided into three main sections: Padmavati Machi, Suvela Machi, and Sanjeevani Machi, each with its own unique features.

Rajgad Fort is renowned for its massive fortifications, ancient gateways, and historical structures, including the Padmavati Temple and the remains of the royal palace. The fort also offers breathtaking views of the Sahyadri mountain range.

Today, Rajgad Fort is a popular trekking destination and a symbol of Maratha pride. Its historical significance and natural beauty make it a must-visit site for history enthusiasts and nature lovers.
